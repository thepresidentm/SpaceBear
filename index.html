Space Junk?

Space junk is the involuntary result of more than six decades of human intiatives on space, most of it comes from desintegration, explosion or colision of the approximately 8950 satelites put in orbit.

Did you know...?

One piece of debris the size of 3 mm has 2 Joules of kinetic energy, the same energy than a bullet!

How does it affect us?

We use the space to deploy spaceships that allows us to study our planet and the universe. The space is useful for global communications and navigation, these are important for a high vierity of things like air traffic management or even Uber. Low orbit is essential for space exploration missions, space junk puts in danger human operated missions.

Kessler Syndrome

Kessler demonstrated that once the amount of debris in a particular orbit reaches the critical mass, the collision cascade begins even if no additional objects are launched into the orbit. Once the collision cascade begins, the risk to satellites and spacecraft increases until the orbit is no longer usable.
